Saleh Hasan Oni
Oni Hasan (born August 1, 1985) is a Bangladeshi musician,
guitarist, and songwriter from Dhaka, Bangladesh.
Saleh Hasan Oni was born on 1 August 1985; in Faridpur. He is of
Bangladeshi descent and comes from a family of greater Comilla
district. He is the eldest child of the family, with two younger
brothers, Sunny and Sayem Hasan. His father Mr. Kamrul Hasan
is a textile engineer and his mother Ruma Hasan is a home maker.
His father played classical instruments and the two younger
brothers are guitarists as well.
Oni Hasan is recognized for his commitment to music, investing
substantial time in practicing, learning, and imparting his
knowledge of the guitar through teaching. Notably, he is known
for leading a health-conscious lifestyle, choosing to abstain from
alcohol and smoking. He has got two tattoos on his right arm, a
star which is his favorite symbol and absolute perfection written
in Japanese under it. He believes everything he does, be it music or
personal life related, should be done with so much dedication that
it turns out to be absolutely perfect.
[1]
Being a self-taught musician, Oni Hasan started playing the guitar
at the age of 18. He drew inspiration from guitarists like Dimebag
Darrell, Yngwie Malmsteen, Slash, and Paul Gilbert.
His career began in the underground music scene, gradually progressing from modest beginnings to greater
achievements. Initially, he joined the band Illusive Dreams and performed his first live gig at RCC, a
significant venue for musicians in Bangladesh. Subsequently, he received invitations to play with various
underground bands, including Dethrow, Warfaze, Art Cell, Stentorian, and Aurthohin, all at a young age,
only three years after learning to play the guitar.
After perceiving limited progress with his band, he made the decision to leave and was subsequently
invited to join the band Metal Maze for a brief period. Later, in late 2005, he became a member of the band
Vibe and participated in recording their debut album "Chena Jogot."

Bangladeshi Guitarist Oni Hasan
His major breakthrough came when he became a lead guitarist and
composer for the renowned Bangladeshi heavy metal band,
Warfaze. During his tenure with the band, he made significant
contributions to albums such as "Pothchola" and "Shotto."
Throughout his journey, the individual's dedication, talent, and hard
work significantly influenced his rise from an underground
musician to a respected figure within the Bangladeshi music scene,
particularly in the heavy metal genre.

Band Warfaze

He was asked to join Warfaze by Ibrahim Ahmed Kamal, founder and lead guitarist of Warfaze in late
2007. He joined Warfaze with the line-up- Kamal on the Guitars, Balam on the voice, Roger on the Bass,
Shams on the Keys and Tipu on the drums. Oni did his first show with Warfaze in the beginning of 2008
with that line up. That lineup soon broke as Balam had to leave for his solo career and Mizan came back
again as the main voice. They released Warfaze's 7th album Pothchola in the year 2008. Pothchola was a
remake of some old songs mainly focusing on the new generation and the album also consisted of two new
tracks (Omanush, and Tomakey). Pothchola song list (1. Boshey Achi, 2. Tomakey, 3. Omanush, 4. Obak
Bhalobasha, 5. Hotasha, 6. Nei Proyojon, 7. Shadhikar, 8. Joto Durey, 9. DHushor Manchitro, 10. Asha,
11. Shomoe, 12. Ashamajik, 13. Pothchola. ) Warfaze Pothchola won the 6th City Cell Channel I Award in
2010.
(Lalon) (2011)

and Habib had songs as well. Songs they covered were: Khachar Bhitor, Jaat Galo and Aarshi Nogor.
(Shotto) (2012)
In 2011, he had to leave the country for his higher education in the UK, at Keele University to study
Marketing and Music Technology. He came back in March 2012, taking a leave from the university to
record the 8th Warfaze album. Warfaze's 8th Album got released in Oct 2012 with 8 brand new tracks and
one unreleased track. He has two compositions of his own in the album: Naa and Purnota. Both the
